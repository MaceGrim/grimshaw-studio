## Tribal AI / Indigenous Perspective

**Context:** Why Indigenous communities should care about AI sovereignty

> It's annoying to have to talk about AI. It's everywhere even when we don't want it to be, but it's also important to intentionally discuss what AI could mean for our communities and our future. The cons of AI are well understood, there's IP theft, there's a use of electricity and water, but what about the benefits? With responsible deployment, AI can be a huge boon for our resource strapped communities and Indigenous people are well positioned to lead us all toward a future in which AI works with humanity to solve our most important problems instead of accelerating us towards a climate cliff.

**Voice notes:**
- Acknowledges fatigue before diving in
- Balances cons (IP theft, resources) with opportunity
- "Resource strapped" - practical, not academic
- Indigenous as leaders, not recipients
- Stakes without breathlessness

## Enterprise AI / Consulting

**Context:** How to approach AI adoption with skeptical teams

> AI projects live and die in the trenches. It can be a combination of fatigue from the barrage of tools that are constantly being introduced, or a lack of clarity on "what does this thing actually do for us?". AI systems that amplify workflows; that make a sales-person feel more informed about their potential client, or accelerate developers towards larger amounts of better code, are what matters. If you're starting with AI, you want your AI outputs right alongside your most trusted performers. They'll be able to tell you exactly where the tool isn't working, and where they're finding meaningful improvement within their own workflows.

**Voice notes:**
- "Live and die in the trenches" - implementation focus
- Names real blockers: fatigue, unclear value
- Concrete examples: sales-person, developers
- Actionable: "alongside your most trusted performers"
- Human-centered framing
