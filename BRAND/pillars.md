# Content Pillars

Four lanes of content. Each serves a different audience and purpose, but all share the same voice.

## Enterprise AI / Agents

**Audience:** CTOs, VP Engineering, AI leads, technical decision-makers

**What this covers:**
- Production AI systems
- Architecture decisions
- Practical implementation
- What actually works vs. hype

**Example topics:**
- The "trusted performer test" - validating AI by putting it alongside your best people
- Prototype to production - the demo worked, now what?
- Agent architectures that ship - what's working in production right now

**Tone:** Serious, practical, grounded in real engagements

---

## Tribal AI

**Audience:** Tribal leaders, allies, tech folks interested in Indigenous perspectives

**What this covers:**
- AI sovereignty and self-determination
- Technology stewardship
- Capacity building
- Grounded perspectives on AI in communities

**Example topics:**
- AI sovereignty & data ownership - who owns tribal data, how AI trains on it
- Building local expertise - training tribal members, not creating dependencies
- Practical AI tools - grants, language preservation, administrative automation

**Tone:** Accessible, honest about challenges, Indigenous-led framing (not savior)

---

## Technical Deep Dives

**Audience:** Practitioners, peers, people who appreciate craft

**What this covers:**
- Multi-part series on hard problems
- Building things from scratch
- Real project breakdowns

**Example topics:**
- Transformers from scratch - understanding the architecture by building it
- Agent systems internals - how agent architectures actually work under the hood
- Data pipelines at scale - real-world data engineering, not toy examples

**Tone:** Technical, thorough, assumes competence

---

## Experiments / Fun

**Audience:** Everyone - demonstrates range and personality

**What this covers:**
- Playful technical projects
- Simulations and visualizations
- Sports analytics
- "What if?" explorations

**Example topics:**
- RimWorld AI agents - can we build increasingly complex colony managers?
- Sports analytics deep dives
- Visualizing airplane boarding strategies - which method actually loads fastest?
- Automatic paper reproducers - can AI replicate research papers from scratch?

**Tone:** Playful, curious, "let's find out"

---

## How This Supports Speaking

This content builds toward $10k+ speaking engagements targeting enterprise AI decision-makers. Each pillar feeds the speaking pipeline differently:

1. **Enterprise AI** → Establishes credibility with CTOs and VP-level decision-makers who book speakers. Content here doubles as proof of expertise for conference organizers evaluating speaker submissions.
2. **Tribal AI** → Unique perspective no one else has; a genuine differentiator on any keynote stage. Conference organizers want speakers who bring something attendees can't get elsewhere.
3. **Deep Dives** → Proves technical depth; not just a talker. When a conference committee Googles you, these show you can go beyond surface-level takes.
4. **Experiments** → Shows personality and range; memorable. The speaker who built RimWorld AI agents is more interesting than another "AI strategy" slide deck.

The combination - enterprise credibility + Indigenous lens + technical chops + human personality - is the positioning. No one else sits at this intersection, and that's what commands premium speaking fees.
