# The Honest Cost of AI Prototypes

## Scope

An Enterprise AI pillar post that breaks down what it actually takes to build AI prototypes for enterprise clients. Not the vendor pitch version -- the real version. Covers time investment, iteration cycles, expectation management, and the gap between "demo works" and "this is useful." Written from direct consulting experience at Ode Partners.

Target length: 1500-2000 words.
Format: Blog post with embedded visualization.
Pillar: Enterprise AI / Agents.

## Audience

Primary: Enterprise AI decision-makers (CTOs, VP Engineering, AI leads) who are evaluating or have already started AI prototype projects. These are people who have been burned by the gap between a polished vendor demo and the messy reality of their own data and processes.

Secondary: Technical practitioners who build prototypes and need language to set expectations with their leadership.

## Key Points

1. **The timeline illusion** -- Vendor demos take 2 hours to build. Real prototypes that touch your data, your edge cases, and your workflows take 6-12 weeks minimum. The demo is not the prototype.

2. **The iteration tax** -- Every prototype goes through 3-5 major pivots. The first version almost never survives contact with real users. Budget for iteration, not just initial build.

3. **Hidden costs nobody talks about** -- Data cleaning, stakeholder alignment, integration testing, change management prep. The model is maybe 20% of the actual work.

4. **Managing the expectation gap** -- Leadership sees the demo and expects production next quarter. How to communicate realistic timelines without killing enthusiasm.

5. **What a healthy prototype process looks like** -- A practical framework: the trusted performer test, staged rollout, honest evaluation criteria.
